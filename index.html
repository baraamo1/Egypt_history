Egypt civilization :

Ancient Egyptian civilization started at 3100 BC when Mina united upper Egypt and lower Egypt , The ancient Egyptian was divided into 30 dynasties and several major historical periods , those dynasties and periods are :

Early Dynastic Period ( dynasties 1-2 )

Started at:

3100 BC when king Mina united the upper Egypt and lower Egypt



Ended at :

2686 BC due to the stable and well-organized kingdom. This marked the beginning of the Old Kingdom under the Third Dynasty



Major achievements :

  • The unification of Upper Egypt and lower Egypt

  • The creation of the first centralized government

  • The organization of the Egyptian army

  • Making Memphis(minf) the capital of Egypt

  • The development of the hieroglyphic writing system

  • the construction of first royal tombs


The main kings :

1-Mina

King Mina is the first king in Early Dynastic Period and he is the creator of the first dynasties , he had many achievements :

  • He united the upper Egypt and lower Egypt

  • He founded the ancient Egyptian state

  • He organized the administration of the country and divided it into administrative regions

  • He promoted the trade in Egypt

  • He established the first strong central government

  • He founded Memphis (minf) and made it the capital of Egypt

2-Aha

King Aha is the second king in Early Dynastic Period , he ruled after king mina , he had many achievements :

  • He maintained the unity of upper Egypt and lower Egypt

  • He strengthened the central government

  • He built temples and administrative buildings

  • He led military campaigns into Nubia to protect southern Egypt

  • He improved the administration of the kingdom

  • He supported trading within Egypt and with neighboring regions

3-Den

King Den is the fifth king in first dynasty in Early Dynastic Period , he is one of the important kings , his achievements :

  • He improved the government administration

  • He organized the tax system

  • He strengthened the army

  • He led military campaigns in Sinai

  • He wore the double crown as a symbol of the united Egypt

4-Khasekhemwy

King Khasekhemwy is the last king in the second dynasty in Early Dynastic Period , he is one of the important kings , his achievements :

  • He reunified Egypt after internal conflicts

  • He ended rebellions and restored stability

  • He built temples and large monuments

  • He Paved the way for the Old Kingdom

The old kingdom period
(The age of the pyramid builders)
(dynasties 3-4-5-6)

Started at:

2686 BC



Ended at :

2181 BC due to the central government became weak, local governors gained more power, and economic problems and low Nile floods led to the collapse of the kingdom



Major achievements :

  • Built the pyramids, including the Great Pyramid

  • Built the Step Pyramid of Saqqara, the first pyramid in history

  • Developed stone architecture

  • Strengthened the central government and administration.

  • Built temples and funerary complexes

  • Expanded trade and sent expeditions to Sinai, Nubia, and Punt

  • The Pyramid Texts appeared for the first time during the reign of Unas

The late Period
(dynasties 26-27-28-29-30)

Started at:

664 BC



Ended at :

332 BC , It was the last period of native Egyptian rule before the Greek (Ptolemaic) Period



Major achievements :

  • Reunified Egypt

  • Expanded the Egyptian Empire into Nubia and the Levant

  • Restored stability

  • Built and restored temples.

  • Strengthened the army and navy

  • Expanded trade with the Greeks and Phoenicians

  • Revived Egyptian art and architecture

  • Started a canal linking the Nile to the Red Sea
